   - EN: How many different 3-letter arrangements can be made from the letters A, B, C, D, E if no letter can be repeated?
   - FR: Combien d'arrangements de 3 lettres différentes peut-on former avec les lettres A, B, C, D, E si aucune lettre ne peut se répéter?
   - Choices: A) 30  B) 45  C) 60  D) 120
   - Hint: This is a permutation: P(n, r) = n! ÷ (n − r)! — like P(4, 2) = 4! ÷ 2! = 24 ÷ 2 = 12.
   - Steps:
     - P(5, 3) = 5! ÷ (5 − 3)!
     - = 5! ÷ 2!
     - = 120 ÷ 2 = 60.
   - Answer: 60

10. **[Hard] Polynomial Long Division**
    - EN: Divide (x³ + 3x² − x − 3) by (x − 1). What is the quotient?
    - FR: Diviser (x³ + 3x² − x − 3) par (x − 1). Quel est le quotient?
    - Choices: A) x² + 4x + 3  B) x² + 3x − 1  C) x² + 2x + 1  D) x² + 4x − 3
    - Hint: Use synthetic or long division — bring down coefficients, multiply and subtract at each step, like dividing x³ + 5x² + 6x by x gives x² + 5x + 6.
    - Steps:
      - Divide x³ by x to get x². Multiply: x²(x − 1) = x³ − x². Subtract: 3x² − (−x²) = 4x².
      - Bring down: 4x² − x. Divide 4x² by x = 4x. Multiply: 4x(x − 1) = 4x² − 4x. Subtract: −x − (−4x) = 3x.
      - Bring down: 3x − 3. Divide 3x by x = 3. Multiply: 3(x − 1) = 3x − 3. Remainder = 0.
      - Quotient: x² + 4x + 3.
    - Answer: x² + 4x + 3
