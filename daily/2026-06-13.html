Grade 1 Problems

  1. [Easy] Counting Hockey Pucks

    • EN: There are 7 hockey pucks on the ice. 3 more are added. How many pucks are there now?
    • FR: Il y a 7 rondelles de hockey sur la glace. On en ajoute 3. Combien y a-t-il de rondelles maintenant ?
    • Choices: A) 9 B) 10 C) 11 D) 8
    • Hint: Count on from 7.
    • Steps:
      • Start at 7.
      • Count 3 more: 8, 9, 10.
    • Answer: 10

  2. [Easy] Maple Leaves

    • EN: Mia finds 5 maple leaves. She gives 2 to her friend. How many does she have left?
    • FR: Mia trouve 5 feuilles d'érable. Elle en donne 2 à son amie. Combien lui en reste-t-il ?
    • Choices: A) 4 B) 2 C) 3 D) 7
    • Hint: Take away means subtract.
    • Steps:
      • Start with 5 leaves.
      • Subtract 2: 5 − 2 = 3.
    • Answer: 3

  3. [Easy] Skip Counting by 2s

    • EN: Count by 2s: 2, 4, 6, __, 10. What is the missing number?
    • FR: Compte par 2 : 2, 4, 6, __, 10. Quel est le nombre manquant ?
    • Choices: A) 7 B) 9 C) 8 D) 6
    • Hint: Add 2 to the number before the blank.
    • Steps:
      • The number before the blank is 6.
      • 6 + 2 = 8.
    • Answer: 8

  4. [Easy] Pennies in a Jar

    • EN: Luca has 9 pennies. He finds 4 more. How many pennies does he have in all?
    • FR: Luca a 9 sous. Il en trouve 4 de plus. Combien de sous a-t-il en tout ?
    • Choices: A) 12 B) 14 C) 13 D) 5
    • Hint: Add the two groups together.
    • Steps:
      • Start at 9.
      • Count on 4 more: 10, 11, 12, 13.
    • Answer: 13

  5. [Medium] Snowflakes on the Window

  6. [Medium] Dimes and Nickels

  7. [Medium] Skip Counting by 5s

    • EN: Count by 5s: 5, 10, 15, __, 25. What number is missing?
    • FR: Compte par 5 : 5, 10, 15, __, 25. Quel nombre manque ?

  8. [Medium] Birds on a Fence

    • EN: There are 8 birds on a fence. 5 fly away, then 3 more land. How many birds are on the fence now?
    • FR: Il y a 8 oiseaux sur une clôture. 5 s'envolent, puis 3 autres atterrissent. Combien y a-t-il d'oiseaux maintenant ?

  9. [Hard] Apples at the Market

    • EN: A farmer has 17 apples. He sells some and has 9 left. How many did he sell?
    • FR: Un fermier a 17 pommes. Il en vend quelques-unes et il lui en reste 9. Combien en a-t-il vendu ?

  10. [Hard] Counting to 100

    • EN: Sasha counts by 10s starting at 10. She stops at 80. How many times did she count?

Grade 2 Problems

  1. [Easy] Adding Stickers

    • EN: Emma has 23 stickers. She gets 14 more. How many stickers does she have?
    • FR: Emma a 23 autocollants. Elle en reçoit 14 de plus. Combien en a-t-elle ?
    • Choices: A) 35 B) 38 C) 37 D) 36
    • Hint: Add the ones first, then the tens.
    • Steps:
      • 3 + 4 = 7 ones.
      • 20 + 10 = 30 tens. Total: 37.
    • Answer: 37

  2. [Easy] Telling Time

    • EN: The clock shows 3:30. What time is it?
    • FR: L'horloge indique 3 h 30. Quelle heure est-il ?
    • Choices: A) 3:00 B) 4:30 C) 3:30 D) 2:30
    • Hint: The short hand points to 3; the long hand points to 6.
    • Steps:
      • Short hand at 3 means the hour is 3.
      • Long hand at 6 means 30 minutes. Time is 3:30.
    • Answer: 330

    (Note: Answer represented as 330 = 3 hours 30 minutes in HHMM format; for single-number answer the problem is reframed below.)

    (Reframed for single numeric answer):

    • Hint: "Half past" means halfway around the clock.
    • Steps:
      • Half an hour = 30 minutes.

    • Answer: 30

Grade 3 Problems

  1. [Easy] Multiplication by 5

    • EN: There are 5 bags of maple candies. Each bag has 4 candies. How many candies are there in all?
    • FR: Il y a 5 sacs de bonbons à l'érable. Chaque sac contient 4 bonbons. Combien y a-t-il de bonbons en tout ?
    • Choices: A) 18 B) 20 C) 24 D) 9
    • Hint: Use the 5 times table.
    • Steps:
      • 5 × 4 = 20.
    • Answer: 20

  2. [Easy] Simple Division

    • EN: 12 hockey cards are shared equally among 3 friends. How many cards does each friend get?
    • FR: 12 cartes de hockey sont partagées également entre 3 amis. Combien de cartes chaque ami reçoit-il ?
    • Choices: A) 3 B) 5 C) 4 D) 6
    • Hint: Think: 3 × ? = 12.
    • Steps:
      • 12 ÷ 3 = 4.
    • Answer: 4

  3. [Easy] Fractions — Half

    • EN: A pizza is cut into 4 equal slices. You eat 2 slices. What fraction of the pizza did you eat?
    • FR: Une pizza est coupée en 4 tranches égales. Tu en manges 2. Quelle fraction de la pizza as-tu mangée ?
    • Choices: A) 1 B) 3 C) 2 D) 4
    • Hint: 2 out of 4 equal parts simplifies to one half — write the numerator only.
    • Steps:
      • You ate 2 out of 4 slices.
      • 2/4 = 1/2; the numerator of the simplified fraction is 1.
    • Answer: 1

    (Reframed for unambiguous single number):

Grade 4 Problems

  1. [Easy] Multiplication Tables

    • EN: What is 7 × 8?
    • FR: Combien font 7 × 8 ?
    • Choices: A) 54 B) 63 C) 56 D) 48
    • Hint: Think of 7 groups of 8.
    • Steps:
      • 7 × 8 = 56.
    • Answer: 56

  2. [Easy] Decimal Place Value

    • EN: What is the value of the digit 4 in the number 3.47? (Answer in hundredths — how many hundredths?)
    • FR: Quelle est la valeur du chiffre 4 dans le nombre 3,47 ? (Répondre en dixièmes — combien de dixièmes ?)
    • Choices: A) 4 B) 40 C) 400 D) 0
    • Hint: The first digit after the decimal point is in the tenths place.
    • Steps:
      • 3.47 → 3 ones, 4 tenths, 7 hundredths.
      • The digit 4 is in the tenths place: its value is 4 tenths.
    • Answer: 4

  3. [Easy] Area of a Rectangle

    • EN: A hockey rink section is 9 m long and 6 m wide. What is its area?
    • FR: Une section de patinoire de hockey mesure 9 m de long et 6 m de large. Quelle est son aire ?
    • Choices: A) 30 B) 54 C) 63 D) 45
    • Hint: Area = length × width.
    • Steps:
      • 9 × 6 = 54 m².
    • Answer: 54

  4. [Easy] Comparing Fractions

    • EN: Which fraction is larger: 3/4 or 2/4? Enter the numerator of the larger fraction.
    • FR: Quelle fraction est la plus grande : 3/4 ou 2/4 ? Entrez le numérateur de la plus grande fraction.
    • Choices: A) 2 B) 4 C) 1 D) 3
    • Hint: When denominators are the same, compare numerators.
    • Steps:
      • Both fractions have denominator 4.
      • 3 > 2, so 3/4 is larger. Numerator = 3.
    • Answer: 3

  5. [Medium] Long Division with Remainder

    • EN: 47 students are split into groups of 5. How many students are left over (the remainder)?
    • FR: 47 élèves sont divisés en groupes de 5. Combien d'élèves restent-ils (le reste) ?
    • Choices: A) 3 B) 9 C) 2 D) 7
    • Hint: Find how many complete groups fit, then subtract.
    • Steps:
      • 5 × 9 = 45.
      • 47 − 45 = 2 remainder.
    • Answer: 2

  6. [Medium] Elapsed Time

    • EN: A hockey game starts at 6:15 PM and ends at 8:45 PM. How many minutes long is the game?
    • FR: Un match de hockey commence à 18 h 15 et se termine à 20 h 45. Combien de minutes dure le match ?
    • Choices: A) 120 B) 140 C) 130 D) 150
    • Hint: Find the difference between the start and end time.
    • Steps:
      • From 6:15 to 8:15 = 2 hours = 120 minutes.
      • From 8:15 to 8:45 = 30 more minutes.
      • 120 + 30 = 150 minutes.
    • Answer: 150

  7. [Medium] Decimal Operations

    • EN: A sweater costs $14.75. A scarf costs $8.50. How much do they cost together (in dollars, numeric only)?
    • FR: Un chandail coûte 14,75 $. Une écharpe coûte 8,50 $. Quel est leur coût total (en dollars, chiffres seulement) ?
    • Choices: A) 22.25 B) 23.25 C) 23.00 D) 24.25
    • Hint: Line up the decimal points and add.
    • Steps:
      • $14.75 + $8.50.
      • 75 + 50 = 125 cents = $1.25 carry $1.
      • 14 + 8 + 1 = $23. Total: $23.25.
    • Answer: 23.25

  8. [Medium] Multi-Step Area

    • EN: A rectangular classroom is 8 m long and 7 m wide. A bookshelf covers 4 m² of the floor. How many square metres of floor are uncovered?
    • FR: Une salle de classe rectangulaire mesure 8 m de long et 7 m de large. Une bibliothèque occupe 4 m² du plancher. Combien de mètres carrés de plancher sont dégagés ?
    • Choices: A) 52 B) 60 C) 56 D) 44
    • Hint: Find total area first, then subtract.
    • Steps:
      • Area = 8 × 7 = 56 m².
      • 56 − 4 = 52 m² uncovered.
    • Answer: 52

  9. [Hard] Long Division

    • EN: 98 bottles of maple syrup are packed into crates of 8. How many complete crates can be filled?
    • FR: 98 bouteilles de sirop d'érable sont emballées dans des caisses de 8. Combien de caisses complètes peut-on remplir ?
    • Choices: A) 11 B) 13 C) 12 D) 10
    • Hint: Divide 98 by 8 and ignore the remainder.
    • Steps:
      • 8 × 12 = 96.
      • 98 − 96 = 2 remainder.
      • 12 complete crates.
    • Answer: 12

  10. [Hard] Two-Step Decimals and Area

    • EN: Liam buys 6 notebooks at $3.25 each. He pays with a $20 bill. How much change does he receive (in dollars)?
    • FR: Liam achète 6 cahiers à 3,25 $ chacun. Il paie avec un billet de 20 $. Combien de monnaie reçoit-il (en dollars) ?
    • Choices: A) 0.50 B) 1.50 C) 2.50 D) 3.50
    • Hint: First find the total cost, then subtract from $20.
    • Steps:
      • 6 × $3.25 = $19.50.
      • $20.00 − $19.50 = $0.50.
    • Answer: 0.50

    • FR: Un terrain de camping loue des canots à 24 $ par jour. Combien cela coûte-t-il pour 5 jours ?

    • Hint: Multiply 24 by 5.
    • Steps:

    • Answer: 120

    • FR: Zoé mange 2/8 d'une tarte et son frère en mange 3/8. Quelle fraction de la tarte a été mangée au total ? (Entrez le numérateur.)

    • Hint: Add the numerators; keep the denominator the same.
    • Steps:

    • Answer: 5

  3. [Easy] Decimal Operations

    • EN: A litre of maple syrup costs $12.40. You buy 2 litres. How much do you pay?
    • FR: Un litre de sirop d'érable coûte 12,40 $. Tu en achètes 2 litres. Combien paies-tu ?

  4. [Easy] 25% of a Number

    • FR: Un chandail de hockey coûte 80 $ à l'origine. Il est en solde avec 25 % de rabais. Quel est le montant du rabais ?

    • Steps:

  5. [Medium] Multi-Digit Division

  9. [Hard] Multi-Step Fractions and Decimals

    • EN: Maya has $15.60. She spends 1/4 of it on a snack. How much does she have left (in dollars)?

    • Hint: Find 1/4 of $15.60 first, then subtract.
    • Steps:

      • $15.60 − $3.90 = $11.70.

  10. [Hard] Volume and Percentage

    • EN: A fish tank has a volume of 120 L. It is 75% full. How many litres of water are in the tank?
    • FR: Un aquarium a un volume de 120 L. Il est rempli à 75 %. Combien de litres d'eau contient-il ?
    • Choices: A) 80 B) 100 C) 90 D) 75
    • Hint: 75% = 50% + 25%. Find each part separately.
    • Steps:
      • 50% of 120 = 60.
      • 25% of 120 = 30.
      • 60 + 30 = 90 L.
